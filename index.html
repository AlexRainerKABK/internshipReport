Internship Report by Alex Rainer on the experience at StudioStudio in Gent.

I think it gave me a strong insight into how a one-person studio operates and how stressful it can become with sudden assignments and last-minute changes that still need to meet the deadline. I also wasn’t expecting how much he trusted us and treated us as full employees, fully involved in every step of each project and even making some decisions ourselves. On the other hand, we worked more hours than we had agreed, which we accepted, but we didn’t always feel that extra effort was appreciated. It also showed me how I don’t want to work: he seemed very stressed and often overwhelmed, and the workload spilled into his free time. That’s not something I want to sacrifice, and I don’t think it’s sustainable. I’ll still take some things with me, like how he set up his studio at home. It’s convenient, but it also blurs the line between home and work, making it hard to switch off mentally. I also reflected on the use of AI for coding and was surprised by what you can achieve without deep coding knowledge. You can create digital tools tailored to your practice or your clients. But you’re still limited by your own understanding: if the code becomes too complex and you don’t know what to ask the AI, you can’t pinpoint issues, and it may start to hallucinate. AI is only as good as the questions you ask it, and it’s easy to get carried away by quick results without a clear intention.

Studio Studio Link Tree: This was my first and very short assignment after arriving in Gent. A website that displayed all his social links on a visible dynamic greed that changes on page refresh its dimensions.

3Sec Graphic: This was an assignment where Studio Studio was asked to participate at the 3sec Gallery. Since the deadline was very short and the assignment was to create 60 posters we decided to create a tool that can help us to design the posters. We collectively brainstormed on the functionality and restrictions on the tool that aligned with the concept of Slow and Fast or Close and Far, which arrived from the location of the 3sec Gallery. I’m enthusiastic about coding but it was also quite challenging for me because of the short deadline we had. But in the end I was really surprised on how well it worked to code this tool with the help of AI. The tool allows you to control layout, scale, spacing, rhythm, density, and canvas size while remaining cohesive. And it’s also available for the public to use.

Typo Typo experimental type foundry: For this assignment I created a Web shop for the Typo Typo foundry. First I created a research report on how other type foundries display their typefaces and from there we decided on a design concept arriving from the name Typo Typo to create a split panel view. Where you have a left panel with all the visual information and on the right the more technical details. And so the entire navigation and flow of the website works with this split panel view.
